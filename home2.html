Killing Time: Designing a Short Animation

We started work on the original storyline back in October 2004. We had been bandying about numerous scenarios that could be developed further and Killing Time actually evolved as the result of the development for a completely different character… There was a prop within the home set and one suggestion lead to another and before we knew it, a lonely old man we’ve come to know as Ernest Gittings was created before our very eyes. As you may notice from the original start date the animation has been an incredibly slow process, however we only started the production early in 2006 as we spent all the previous time refining and honing the story.
We started with the character design as this was most fun and then moved onto researching the architecture associated with different time periods from the late 1800’s to 1950 and the furniture that went with it. We had a vision of quite a bleak and cold cityscape as a background and used local buildings and building materials as a reference, we also had a hankering towards the old tenement buildings from Glasgow that we both felt had suitable qualities and a certain character.

The next big progression was a production of a 2d, and then 3d animatic so that we were able to adjust the real dynamics of the camera movement and composition in each scene until we were happy. We also developed an emotional timing sheet for each shot so that each camera shot and scene was delivering what we needed it to.

Killing Time: Production

After reaching agreement that what we had was right, we had to convert each sketched character, building, piece of furniture and prop into a 3D representation which move correctly and looked as if it belonged. No small task.

Our learning curve has been incredibly steep but it has been worth the substantial hiccups along the way, and it is only now that we can begin to let everyone know how we’re doing as we can see light at the end of the tunnel and the completion is becoming a reality.

We have been asked so many times how the project is going and it is hard to describe in exact terms what each step of progression means, but it’s close enough to share now as we didn’t want to be perceived as another online project that fails to materialize or promise to be something that never reaches its completion.
